The Rise of the Church of Five

As Witnessed by Known, the Silent Record

There are moments in time that echo louder than others, threads of history that shimmer brighter in the grand weave. I was there, though unseen, as I always am. I do not speak. I do not interfere. I only observe and remember. Such is my purpose — to Know.

Long after mortals had claimed their corners of the world but before the Holy Knights etched their names into it, faith in the divine was a faint whisper. The gods had withdrawn, their forms lost to mortal eyes, and only the gentle traces of their angels remained — flickers in dreams, omens in still water, the hush of breath when the soul brushes the sacred.

In this age of searching hearts, the Church of Five came to be — a devotion not to individual gods, but to the Five Pillars of Divinity: Order, Life, Death, Time, and Will.

And I was there. I watched as whispers turned into voices. As dreams became belief. As belief forged fire.

It was Destiny, the Angel of Fate, and Prai, the Angel of Worship, whose echoes carried strongest through the aether. They did not walk among mortals often, but when they did, the world trembled — not in fear, but in awakening.

The First Prophet

I watched her for some time before she ever heard the angels. Ilessa Veran, wanderer, listener, woman of quiet strength. She had known hardship: famine’s claw, war’s howl, the emptiness left by loss. And yet, her gaze was clear. Her steps were sure. She had no throne, no army — only words, offered freely to those in need.

It was at the edge of a quiet forest where fate entwined with purpose. That night, I stood unseen among the trees as she slept. And I watched as Destiny revealed himself in her dream — silver threads spinning in the void, a wheel of fate, every line humming with lives entwined.

I offered her the first thread of the tapestry to come. A sliver of divine light, coiled like memory in his palm.

"Mortal," Destiny said, his voice soft but immovable. "Do you see it? The weave of fate that binds all to their purpose?"

She answered with honesty, not arrogance. "I see it... but I do not understand it."

"Few do," he said, amused. "But you will. You have seen too much, suffered too much, and now it is time to guide others. The Five see you, mortal. They know your heart, and they have sent me to call you to their purpose."

"Why me?"

"Because your faith has never faltered, even when the world crumbled around you."

As he spoke, I felt the strands of fate respond — the threads bent toward her, shaping new futures. Destiny raised his hand and wove five glowing emblems into the air. I watched as the meaning of each flowed into her heart — Order, Life, Death, Time, Will.

"You will be the voice that calls them to faith. Not in me. Not in the angels. But in the Five who shape all things."

The vision ended, but she awoke changed. I watched her walk from village to village, carrying the message carved into her soul. And I followed.

The Trials of Faith

Not all welcomed her.

In Valthor’s Crown, faith had grown fat on gold. The priests of the old gods clung to their power, deaf to the hunger outside their jeweled walls. Gorvan Marr, the high priest, smelled of incense and fear. I watched as he declared Ilessa a heretic, saw the bruises on her face as she was dragged before the people. He shouted lies atop marble steps. He thought power was loudness.

But truth was already there, whispered in the hearts of the people.

Then I felt it — the shift in air, the curling smoke. A hooded figure stepped forth, eyes glowing like cinders. I knew her instantly, even veiled in flesh. Lucina wore judgment like a cloak.

“If she speaks falsely, then let the gods strike her down now.” A challenge, thrown from her lips.

The crowd hushed. And then Lucina revealed herself — smoke rising, flames dancing at her fingertips.

“But if she speaks true,” she thundered, “then let her judge her accusers as I would judge the wicked.”

Gorvan Marr’s soul cracked under her gaze. I watched it happen. I had seen many fall under the weight of their own sins, but rarely so quickly.

“You speak of gods, but you know nothing of justice,” Lucina said. “You fatten yourself on the labor of the weak. I see your sins, and I name them unforgivable.”

She did not strike him. She did not need to.

When she turned to Ilessa, I saw the spark of divine recognition pass between them.

“Go. Your faith has brought you before judgment, and you have been found worthy.”

Then she was gone, and the city — Valthor’s Crown — was reborn in the hush she left behind.

The Church of Five is Forged

That night, gold left its temple. The people tore down altars to forgotten gods and placed humble candles in their stead. Ilessa, once a wanderer, was now a prophet. Her voice became many. I observed the creation of the first Chapel of the Five.

And then came Prai.

Her arrival was gentle — warmth, not fire. A hum of worship filled the chapel air, and Ilessa knelt in quiet awe.

“You have done well, Ilessa,” Prai said, her tone like morning light. “But your work is not yet done. Mortals need more than words. They need ritual. They need faith made tangible.”

I listened as they wove the beginnings of faith into form — the Rites of the Five, the Sigil of Five Threads, the days of devotion. Mortals, needing structure, were given it. What was once a whisper became a choir.

The First Chosen

Years passed.

I continued to observe.

In Greylight, I watched a comet arc across the sky in five radiant colors. Below it, a child was born — Eron. Marked from birth by the sigil of the Five, he grew into a symbol of living faith. When beasts attacked, it was he who stood alone with a spear of light.

And I knew: he was not alone.

He was the first of many — those who would rise, marked not by bloodline or title, but by conviction. He and others would become the Order of Holy Knights, wielding both steel and purpose.

I remember the first time he spoke the rites before battle.

I remember how the air itself responded.

I have seen countless ages. I know the truth of what happened, not as myth or sermon, but as it truly was. I carry no judgment. I offer no prayers.

But I remember.

I remember Ilessa’s trembling voice.
I remember Destiny’s mask gleaming with purpose.
I remember Prai’s warmth and Lucina’s fire.
I remember the world before faith had shape.
And I remember the moment it finally did.

— Known
