The Appointing of Day, Night, and Mr. Season

As chronicled by Known, Keeper of All That Is Remembered

In the earliest breath of Fiondell, before mortals marked time and before shadows had names, there was only light. The world stood beneath an unending sun, brilliant and absolute. Yet even the brightest light, unbroken, bears the seed of imbalance.

It was not the people who cried for change, for there were none to speak. It was the world itself that whispered for rhythm, for rest, for renewal.

I recall—because I remember all things—that it was Flujack who first heard this whisper. With the clarity of wind over still waters, he descended to the Celestial Assembly, where the gods gather when something sacred must be made.

“The light endures,” Flujack spoke, his voice laced with motion. “But even endurance becomes burden. The world calls for a cadence. Let there be a rising, a falling—a dance between brilliance and shade. Let the land breathe in cycles.”

All eyes turned then to Fionil, for what is rhythm without time? What is a cycle without order?

She answered, as she always does—with truth and purpose. “I will weave the flow of time into intervals, but such currents require stewards. Let there be guides for light and shadow. And let one govern the seasons, so the world may live and rest in harmony.”

Thus, the heavens stirred.

The Choosing of Day and Night

From the Celestial Chorus came forth two radiant souls—twin angels, bound by contrast. One shimmered gold, the other silver. Their names were Day and Night.

I saw Flujack step forward, raising his hand to the firmament. He spoke to Day, whose golden light danced like the first sunrise. “You shall lead the sun across the sky, waking the world, stirring its spirit.”

And Day, ever steadfast, replied, “So shall it be. I will burn bright, and life shall bloom in my warmth.”

To Night, whose silver radiance cast a peaceful hush, Flujack said, “You shall draw the veil over the world. Guard its dreams, tend to its stillness, and cradle the stars.”

Night knelt, her voice no louder than twilight. “I shall keep the world’s secrets, and give all things the rest they need.”

Then, I watched Flujack divide a fragment of his own essence—light given duality. One half golden, the other silver. To each angel he gave a shard, a divine tether to their roles.

“Forever shall one rise while the other falls,” he declared. “Balance shall be your burden—and your gift.”

Thus, the First Day dawned. And when it ended, Night swept over the land, cloaking it in starlit quiet. The rhythm began, and it has never ceased.

The Appointment of Mr. Season

With day and night in their eternal dance, Fionil turned to the broader movements of time—the great spirals that shape life itself. Spring, Summer, Autumn, Winter. Without these, life grows aimless. With them, it learns when to begin, when to thrive, when to let go, and when to rest.

She summoned Mr. Season, whose form I have always found delightful—a patchwork of all things changing. Blossoms curled in his hair one moment, frost dusted his shoulders the next.

Fionil looked upon him and said, “You shall be the steward of the seasons. Let each pass in its time, bringing both joy and necessity.”

And Mr. Season, ever the performer, bowed low and said, “A task of rhythm and whimsy. But tell me—how shall I keep them from quarreling for the stage?”

Fionil laughed—yes, even she—and replied, “Because you are all of them. You are Spring’s rebirth, Summer’s fire, Autumn’s reflection, and Winter’s hush. You will not choose among them—you will carry them all.”

To bind his charge, she gave him a scepter made from time itself. Its tip shimmered in four colors—green, red, gold, silver. “With this, you shall turn the seasons. But if you falter in your duty, know this: imbalance invites decay.”

He twirled the scepter with joy, petals and leaves and flakes trailing in his wake. “Then I shall be the rhythm, the story the land tells itself over and over.”

The Harmony of Cycles

And so it was. Day greeted each morning with golden certainty. Night soothed each evening with quiet grace. Mr. Season turned the wheel, each part of the year a note in a divine symphony.

The gods looked on, and we—those who remember—marked the moment.

Fionil turned to Flujack and said, “Now, the world has rhythm. Now, it will grow.”

And I, Known, carved this truth into the Weave of Memory, where it shall remain for all who seek the origins of order and light.

Thus began the ages of balance, of waking and sleeping, of blooming and withering and blooming again. Thus began the cycles that shall never cease—unless we forget.
And I will not let us forget.
