The Divine Heart of the Feywild: Tellemori, Onigod, and Their Celestial Stewards

While the Feywild is ruled by wild magic and intense emotion, its foundation is shaped by two divine beings: the ancient dreamer Tellemori, god of earth, water, and superstition, and the radiant trickster Onigod, goddess of arcana, illusion, and mischief.

These two gods are not lovers, rivals, or opposites—but rather perfect complements, like river and reflection, or root and lightning. Together, they dreamed the Feywild into being: Tellemori wove its natural soul, and Onigod filled it with wonder, color, and chaos.

Though war exists in the mortal and outer realms, the gods themselves never fight. The divine are bound by ancient concord, and the Feywild is the purest result of that harmony—chaotic in form, but peaceful in purpose.

Tellemori – The Rootfather, the Dreaming Depth

Tellemori is the god of earth, water, superstition, and the old magic beneath all things. He is the stillness before a storm, the rhythm of tides, the whisper of roots beneath your feet. He does not rule with commands or fury—but with patience, presence, and inevitability.

He is not cold or distant. He simply moves slowly, like stone eroding or rivers carving through mountains. Those who listen find his wisdom in lullabies, folk sayings, and the quiet between breaths.

He is not a god of temples or gold, but of rings of stones, cups of spring water, and children's rhymes repeated across generations.

Title: The Rootfather, Stone-Sleeper, the Dreaming Depth

Domains: Earth, Water, Superstition, Endurance

Symbol: A circle of water within a circle of stone

Known For: Unshakable peace; he sees the long path, not the immediate victory.

Where Onigod skips between laughter and lightning, Tellemori simply waits, watches, and grows. And yet—he is deeply fond of her. Her unpredictability does not disturb him; it amuses him, as waves amuse the shoreline.

Together, they created the Feywild not as ruler and rebel, but as co-artists. Where Onigod gave it color, Tellemori gave it shape. Where she gave whimsy, he gave memory.

It is said that when she teases him, he only smiles—that slow, knowing smile of the earth that cannot be rushed.

Onigod – The Laughing Veil, Mistress of Twisting Paths

Onigod is the goddess of arcane artistry, riddles, mischief, and inspired madness. She is the spark behind every strange coincidence, every spell that reshapes the world, every joke that opens the mind. Where Tellemori built the bones of the Feywild, Onigod taught it to dance.

She is adored by trickster spirits, illusionists, fey pranksters, and clever sages. Her joy is in the unexpected, and she is said to play pranks on other gods—not out of malice, but to remind them not to take eternity too seriously.

Her laughter is woven into the Feywild's magic itself. Every playful trick, every strange forest rule, every mischievous bargain the fey make—it all flows from her.

Title: The Laughing Veil, the Mirror-Mother

Domains: Arcana, Trickery, Discovery, Mystery

Symbol: A smiling eye within a spiral

Known For: Speaking only in layered truths; playing tricks even on her worshipers to teach them insight.

She dwells within Tellu-Mountain, not in chambers of stone, but in a shifting arcane maze that rewrites itself each day. Her presence brings starlight, songbirds, and inexplicable riddles.

The Angels of the Feywild Court

Four powerful angels serve or dwell among the gods of the Feywild:

The Lady of Fey

Angel of Tellemori and steward of order in a land of chaos. She ensures rules are obeyed, debts honored, and names remembered. She is grace, courtesy, and veiled power.

Title: The Twilight Queen

Domain: Emotion, Glamour, Story

Symbol: A mirrored mask with open lips

Plantios

Tellemori’s angel of flora, growth, and inevitable renewal. His slow gaze can end an age or nurture a forest. He speaks rarely, but with the gravity of seasons.

Title: The Rooted Flame

Domain: Seasons, Renewal, Decay

Symbol: A burning blossom cupped in leaves

Ioki

Angel of Onigod, and her finest piece of living mischief. Ioki is the divine clown, sage, shapeshifter, and chaos-binder. Though not Tellemori’s angel, Ioki dwells permanently in the Feywild, often seen as the heart of its trickster spirit.

Their bond with the Lady of Fey is legendary: lovers, rivals, mirror-souls—no one is quite sure, least of all the two of them.

Title: The Mask Between, the Laughing Glyph

Domain: Trickery, Arcana, Change

Symbol: A spiral of laughing mouths inside a magic circle

Tellu-Mountain: The Living Divine Palace

At the spiritual center of the Feywild rises Tellu-Mountain, a vast, living mountain whose peak touches the edge of dreams and whose roots burrow into every realm of magic. It is home to Tellemori, Onigod, and their angelic court.

It is not a fortress—it does not need walls. Its protection is harmony, mystery, and deep magic. It is a palace of paradoxes: ever-shifting, yet eternal.

The Deepheart Cavern – Where Tellemori sleeps and breathes. Silence lives here.

The Bloomspire – Plantios’s tower of spiraling vines, always blooming and withering.

The Mirrorhall – Lady of Fey’s domain, where every reflection reveals truth and history.

The Foxlight Pavilion – Ioki’s realm of light, shadow, laughter, and confusion.

The Spiral Maze – Onigod’s shifting temple, where answers only come when questions are asked backward.

Visitors to Tellu-Mountain must answer riddles, sing to trees, or give their names to shadows. Those who try to take from it find themselves gently unmade and remade as something new.

Divine Harmony, Mortal Struggle

Though war rages in mortal lands, and power shifts among kings and cults, the divine realms are held in balance. The gods do not fight; their angels do not fall. They create, nudge, inspire, and redirect.

The Feywild reflects this cosmic truth: that even chaos can serve peace, and that beauty and mystery are forces as strong as fire and steel.

The fey do not fight for conquest. They trick, tempt, confuse, and enchant—for such things, born of Onigod’s laughter and Tellemori’s dreaming, always teach.
