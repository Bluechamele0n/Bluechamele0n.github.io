The Feywild: A Guide to the Realm of the Fae

As compiled by Archsage Maerindor of the Verdant Circle

Introduction

Beyond the veil of the Material World lies a realm not bound by the laws of logic or time. A place of living dream, endless twilight, and primal magic. This is the Feywild—called Elatha by the elves, The Brightlands by druids, and simply the Green World by those wise enough to speak of it in hushed tones.

The Feywild is not just another plane of existence. It is the soul of the natural world, shaped by memory, emotion, and belief. Here, feelings shape the land. Music can tame beasts. And the wrong word may cost your name.

The Nature of the Feywild

Mirrored, Yet Wild

The Feywild mirrors the Material Plane, but its features are exaggerated. Forests are older, skies more colorful, rivers sing, and even shadows shimmer with life. A village in the mortal realm might be echoed in the Feywild as a grove of sentient trees, or a mist-covered ruin ruled by a dreaming spirit.

Emotion as Power

In the Feywild, emotion is a force as real as gravity or fire. A lover’s sorrow might cause the moon to weep rain; a child’s joy may cause flowers to bloom out of season. Powerful fey can shape the world through sheer will and feeling.

The Rule of Words

Words hold power in the Feywild. Promises are binding. Lies are dangerous. To say “I will return” is to make a contract. The fey do not forgive those who break their word, whether through malice or carelessness.

Time and Memory

Time does not move predictably in the Feywild. A traveler might spend a day in its woods and return to find a century passed—or discover no time passed at all. Likewise, memory itself can warp. Mortals who stay too long may forget their past, their name, or even their purpose.

Getting There—and Back

Passage to the Feywild is not undertaken lightly. While powerful magic can open portals, natural crossings are far more common—and far less predictable. These Fey Crossings often appear:

  • Where natural beauty thrives: groves, waterfalls, standing stones.
  • At liminal times: dawn, dusk, solstices, eclipses.
  • During moments of strong emotion: love, fear, grief.

Exiting the Feywild can be much harder—some are trapped for lifetimes unless aided by a native or protected by fey magic.

The Fey Courts

The Seelie Court (The Court of Summer)

Ruled by Queen Titania, the Seelie Court embodies growth, passion, light, and creativity. Its fey are proud, beautiful, and often helpful—though quick to anger or offense.

  • Values: Hospitality, honor, artistry, respect.
  • Common Creatures: Eladrin, dryads, unicorns, satyrs, noble faeries.
  • Warning: To disrespect a Seelie is to invite eternal scorn... or be transformed into a songbird for a hundred years.

The Unseelie Court (The Court of Winter)

The Queen of Air and Darkness leads the Unseelie—fey of shadow, sorrow, and forgotten things. They do not claim to be evil, only true to the balance of life: decay follows growth, loss follows joy.

  • Values: Freedom, cunning, change, retribution.
  • Common Creatures: Hags, redcaps, boggles, shadow spirits, sorrow sprites.
  • Warning: The Unseelie always keep their bargains. You may not like how.

The Gloaming Concord (Court of Twilight) (optional)

A rare and elusive third court said to seek balance between light and shadow. Its members are quiet stewards of fate and memory, intervening only to preserve the greater weave of destiny.

  • Leader: The Masked One, whose name is lost.
  • Role: Observers, arbiters, oracles.

The Inhabitants of the Feywild

  • Eladrin: Elves infused with seasonal magic. Their mood and power shift with the seasons.
  • Pixies & Sprites: Tiny winged fey known for their pranks and charm magic.
  • Hags: Once noble fey, now corrupted by greed and bitterness. Masters of curses and deals.
  • Dryads & Naiads: Spirits bound to trees, rivers, and natural features. Harm their home, and you risk their wrath.
  • Archfey: Semi-divine beings of immense power—rulers, legends, and walking myths. Some grant warlocks power, others seek mortal champions.
  • Talking Animals: Many animals here are intelligent and fey-touched. Never assume a fox is just a fox.

Laws of the Land

Do’s

  • Be respectful. Courtesy is currency.
  • Offer gifts to those you meet—tokens, songs, poetry, or favors.
  • Ask permission before entering a grove, plucking a flower, or drinking from a stream.

Don’ts

  • Never break a promise.
  • Don’t accept food or drink without knowing the cost.
  • Avoid giving your true name. Names are power in the Feywild.
  • Don’t mock the strange—what seems silly may be sacred.

Feywild Effects on Players

  • Memory Drift: Long stays may cause memories to fade or be altered.
  • Time Displacement: Use a d6 to determine time passed in the Material Plane upon return.
  • Mood Manifestation: Intense emotions may cause magic surges, environmental changes, or draw attention from local fey.
  • Dream Influence: Dreams may carry visions from the Feywild or open portals during sleep.

Adventure Hooks for Players

  • You were raised by fey and recently exiled or released.
  • You made a childhood bargain you don’t remember—but the fey do.
  • A loved one vanished into the Feywild, and you seek them.
  • You carry a fey mark—sign of a destiny not fully your own.

The Feywild is not evil. Nor is it good. It is a realm of feeling and story. Of promises, riddles, and beauty too terrible to behold. Treat it with awe, and it may reveal its wonders. Offend it, and you may become one more tale whispered around the fire.
