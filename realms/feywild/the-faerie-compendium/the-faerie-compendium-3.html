The Faerie Codex, Vol. III: Of Beasts, Banquets, and the Dream That Hunts
As Experienced by Corbin Thorne, Reluctant Guest of the Verdant Shadow

On the Creatures Who Dwell Between Dreams

Not all who dwell in the Feywild are people—some are beasts of spirit, instinct, and terrible wisdom. To name them "monsters" would be mortal arrogance; they are older than our fears.

The Whistlehart

A deer-shaped creature with luminous antlers, soft humming breath, and the eyes of an old poet. If it allows you to follow it, you will find safety. If it watches from a distance, you are already lost.

Tellemori’s Favored. They mourn silently when forests die.

Thorn-Mares

Horse-like fey born from broken oaths and forgotten promises. They gallop in silence, leaving trails of briar where they pass. Ride one only if you have no destination—and no fear of what you may become.

Weakness: Bells, laughter, and honest answers.

Murmurtrees

Trees that whisper what they overhear. Their roots stretch between realms, feeding on secrets. Sit beneath them to learn—but never fall asleep, or you will dream another's life and forget your own.

Banquets of Memory and Mist

Dining among the Fey is ritual. Every bite may hold a story; every course, a test.

The Rules of the Table

  1. Do Not Eat Unless Invited – To steal a taste is to bind your fate.

  2. Eat Only What You Understand – Some dishes are not for mortal minds. Some are not food at all.

  3. Share Your Story When Toasted – Refuse, and you will be forgotten by the host, and perhaps the world.

A Seelie feast glimmers with seasonal fruits, glowing honey, and candied petals that shimmer in your mouth. An Unseelie banquet, by contrast, is somber: smoked rootmeats, chilled starlight, and shadow-wine that shows your regrets in its surface.

It is said the Ninefold Trickster serves meals that taste like lost memories, with silverware that murmurs secrets if held to the ear.

The Dream That Hunts

Few speak of it. Fewer return.

There is something deeper in the Feywild—older than Courts, cleverer than angels. It is known as the Dream That Hunts: a predator of unreal logic, drawn to broken stories, oathbreakers, and those who defy Fey law.

It takes no form until it nears, and then becomes what you fear most.

How to Evade It:

Speak no lie.

Break no rhythm in your steps.

Carry a token from each Court (they argue too much to share prey).

And most of all: do not call its name. Not even now.

Those taken are not killed—they are erased. Not even the Murmurtrees remember them.

Traveler’s Final Warnings

Iron Burns, but Honesty Slices Deeper – Fey admire cleverness, not cruelty. Do not confuse the two.

Keep a Pocket Full of Small Truths – They make excellent trade.

Never Thank Them – In their tongue, it means you owe a life-debt.

The Moon May Speak to You – Listen. But answer only in rhyme.

The Feywild is not a place—it is a pattern, a dream layered upon itself. If you walk its paths with respect, you may return wiser. If not… well. I suppose someone else will find your story.

End of Volume III.
